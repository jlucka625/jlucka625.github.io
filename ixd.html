PARK UF


Group project for the Interaction Design course at the University of Florida. Objective of the project was to design an application to help solve the parking issue on campus. After facilitating two focus groups, we discovered that the user needs for the app were space availability, parking lot information (decals and restrictions), location-based suggestions, and app personalization. We created personas, scenarios, and storyboards to fit the discovered user needs. The final phase of the project was to create task flow diagrams and wireframes (which were delegated evenly to each member of the group to create). The paper linked was written by another group member, however, work that I have contributed on the project is present in the paper. This involves some of the user research, personas, scenarios, storyboards, and wirefrimes.

GROOVESHARK CHAT


Group project for the Interaction Design course at the University of Florida. This was a real client-based project with the local Gainesville company, Grooveshark. The objective was to design a chat feature to be integrated into their current interface. The process involved facilitating user and developer focus groups, creating personas, scenarios, storyboards, task flows, and wireframes. Grooveshark design team critiqued our design after finishing the wireframes and provided feedback. It was then the responsibility of the group to revise their task flows and wireframes before creating a high-fidelity prototype that would be displayed publicly to the student body and Grooveshark design team. The paper linked was written by another group member, however, work that I have contributed on the project is present in the paper. This involves some of the user research, personas, scenarios, storyboards, wireframes, and prototypes.
